**5. Accessibility Annotation**
Add a small note on the page explaining the color progression (e.g., "Colors progress from purple (1996) to deep red (2025)") which also helps color-blind users understand the mapping.

CLAUDE: Good question! Let me think about this:

**Blue-to-Red/Orange for colorblindness:**

The issue is that red-green colorblindness (the most common types) makes red and green hard to distinguish. A **blue-to-red/orange** scale could work, but it depends on the specific reds/oranges:

- ✅ **Works well for**: Deuteranopia and Protanopia (red-green blindness) because blue and red/orange are in different color channels
- ❌ **Potential issue**: Tritanopia (blue-yellow blindness, very rare ~0.001%) would struggle with blue-heavy scales

**A simple blue-to-red/orange could be:**
- 1996 (start): Deep Blue (#0033cc or #003399)
- Mid-years: Light Blue → Cyan → Teal
- 2025 (end): Orange-Red → Deep Red (#cc0000)

**Trade-off to consider:**
- More visually striking than Viridis (closer to your current aesthetic with warm reds)
- Still accessible for ~99% of colorblind users (the common types)
- Simpler perceptual journey (cool → warm)
